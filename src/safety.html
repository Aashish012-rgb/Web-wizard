Safety Guidelines

Essential practices to protect yourself and your family in the digital world

Introduction to Digital Safety

Digital safety is about protecting yourself, your information, and your family from online threats. By following these guidelines, you can significantly reduce your risk of becoming a victim of cybercrime.

Key Takeaway: Most cyber attacks can be prevented with basic safety practices. Stay informed, stay vigilant, and help others stay safe.

Password Security

Your passwords are the keys to your digital life. Use these guidelines to create and manage strong passwords:

Create Strong Passwords

Use 12+ characters with uppercase, lowercase, numbers, and symbols. Avoid birthdays or common words.

Unique Passwords

Use different passwords for each important account. If one is breached, others remain secure.

Password Manager

Use a password manager to securely store and generate strong passwords.

Never Share

Never share your passwords, not even with family or close friends. Legitimate services never ask for it.

Change Regularly

Change passwords every 3-6 months, especially for sensitive accounts like email and banking.

Multi-Factor Authentication

Enable 2FA or MFA wherever possible for an extra layer of security.

Personal Information Protection

Be careful about what personal information you share online. Here's what to protect:

  • Social Security/ID Numbers: Never share online; only provide to verified organizations
  • Financial Information: Credit card, bank account, or PayPal details should be shared only on secure websites
  • Location Data: Avoid sharing your real-time location on social media
  • Dates of Birth: This is used in identity theft; keep it private
  • Personal Schedules: Don't post when you'll be away from home
  • Family Information: Don't share details about family members, especially children

Safe Browsing Practices

The way you browse the internet greatly affects your security. Follow these practices:

Website Safety

  • Look for "https://" at the beginning of URLs for secure websites
  • Check for a padlock icon in the address bar
  • Avoid clicking links from unknown sources in emails or messages
  • Type important website addresses directly (e.g., your bank website)
  • Hover over links to see their real destination before clicking

Recognizing Safe Websites

  • The website uses HTTPS encryption (look for the padlock)
  • The domain name matches the organization (not something like "bankofnepal-secure.com")
  • Professional design with no obvious errors or suspicious content
  • Clear contact information and privacy policy
  • No aggressive pop-ups or suspicious advertisements

Email and Messaging Safety

Warning: Email is one of the most common attack vectors. Be extremely careful with email!

Email Safety Tips

  • Never open attachments from unknown senders
  • Don't click links in unsolicited emails
  • Be suspicious of emails asking for personal or financial information
  • Check the sender's actual email address (hover over the name)
  • Official organizations will never ask for passwords via email
  • Watch for poor grammar or spelling (common in phishing emails)
  • Use strong, unique passwords for your email account
  • Set up two-factor authentication for your email

Social Media Safety

Social media is a wonderful tool, but it requires caution:

Social Media Best Practices

  • Review privacy settings on all social media accounts
  • Set your profile to private, not public
  • Be careful about friend requests from strangers
  • Don't accept files or links from people you don't know
  • Think before you post; the internet never forgets
  • Don't share too much personal information
  • Use unique passwords for each social media platform
  • Report and block suspicious or harassing accounts

Mobile Device Safety

Your phone or tablet is a valuable target for cybercriminals:

Keep Updated

Install security updates immediately. They patch critical vulnerabilities.

Use a Lock

Use a PIN, password, or biometric lock (fingerprint, face recognition).

App Safety

Download apps only from official app stores (Google Play, Apple App Store).

WiFi Security

Avoid using public WiFi for sensitive activities like banking. Use a VPN if necessary.

App Permissions

Review and limit app permissions. Does a flashlight app really need access to your contacts?

Regular Cleaning

Uninstall unused apps and clear cache regularly.

Online Banking Safety

Critical: Online banking requires your highest level of security awareness!
  • Only access your bank through the official website or app
  • Never click bank links in emails
  • Use strong passwords and change them regularly
  • Enable all security features your bank offers (alerts, 2FA, etc.)
  • Bank only on devices you trust and with secure internet connections
  • Never give your PIN or security codes to anyone
  • Log out completely when finished
  • Monitor your account regularly for unauthorized transactions

Family and Children Safety

Protecting Your Family

Teach children:

  • Never share personal information online
  • Be respectful and kind in online interactions
  • Tell you if something makes them uncomfortable online
  • Not to meet people they've only met online
  • To recognize and report cyberbullying

Parents and guardians:

  • Monitor your child's online activity appropriately for their age
  • Use parental controls on devices
  • Have open conversations about online safety
  • Know your child's online friends
  • Set screen time limits

What to Do If You're Compromised

If you suspect you've been hacked or compromised:

  1. Change Passwords: Immediately change passwords for compromised accounts and related email
  2. Contact Your Bank: If financial information was exposed, alert your bank immediately
  3. Run Antivirus Scan: Scan your device with updated antivirus software
  4. Monitor Credit: Check credit reports for suspicious activity
  5. File Report: Report the incident to local police or cybercrime bureau
  6. Seek Help: Contact organizations that help cybercrime victims
