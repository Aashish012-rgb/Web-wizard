Your Digital Rights

Understanding your privacy, freedom, and legal protections in Nepal's digital world

Introduction to Digital Rights

Digital rights are an extension of human rights in the digital age. They protect your freedom, privacy, and security online. In Nepal, as in many countries, your digital rights are legally protected, but it's important to understand what they are and how to exercise them.

Key Concept: Your digital rights include the right to privacy, freedom of expression, access to information, and protection from digital harm.

The Right to Privacy

Your personal information, online activities, and communications are protected under Nepal's laws. You have the right to:

  • Keep your personal data private and secure
  • Know what information is being collected about you
  • Control how your data is used and shared
  • Request deletion of your personal data
  • Opt-out of data collection and targeted advertising

Privacy Protection Tips

Protect your privacy by being mindful of what information you share online. Review privacy settings on social media, use strong passwords, and be cautious about public Wi-Fi networks.

Freedom of Expression Online

You have the right to express yourself online without fear of censorship or harassment. However, this right comes with responsibilities:

  • You can share your views, opinions, and ideas freely on the internet
  • You cannot use this freedom to harass, threaten, or defame others
  • Hate speech and incitement to violence are not protected
  • Spreading false information deliberately may have legal consequences

The Right to Access Information

In Nepal, you have the right to access information held by government and public institutions:

  • Right to Information Act enables citizens to request government records
  • You can ask organizations how your data is being used
  • Exceptions apply for national security and personal privacy matters
  • Organizations must respond within specified timeframes

Protection from Cybercrime

Nepal's Cybercrime Law protects you from various online offenses:

Protected Against:

  • Hacking: Unauthorized access to your accounts or devices
  • Phishing: Deceptive attempts to steal your information
  • Online Harassment: Cyberbullying, threats, and intimidation
  • Identity Theft: Using your personal information fraudulently
  • Copyright Infringement: Illegal distribution of your creative work
  • Sexual Exploitation: Non-consensual sharing of intimate images

How to Report Cybercrime in Nepal

If you become a victim of cybercrime, here are the steps you can take:

  1. Document the Crime: Screenshot or record evidence of the incident
  2. Preserve Evidence: Don't delete anything; keep all relevant data
  3. File a Report: Contact the Nepal Police Cyber Bureau or local police station
  4. Report to Platform: Inform the social media or website where the incident occurred
  5. Seek Assistance: Contact cybercrime help organizations for support
Cyber Emergency: Nepal Police Cyber Bureau handles cybercrime cases. You can file a complaint online or visit your nearest police station.

Data Protection and GDPR for Nepal

Nepal is developing its own data protection laws. Here's what you should know:

  • Companies must inform you before collecting personal data
  • You have the right to access your data
  • You can request correction of inaccurate information
  • Organizations must implement data security measures
  • Data breaches must be reported to affected individuals

Your Digital Rights Checklist

Protecting Your Rights

  • Read privacy policies before using services
  • Review and update your privacy settings regularly
  • Use strong, unique passwords for each account
  • Enable two-factor authentication where available
  • Be cautious about sharing personal information
  • Know how to report abuse and cybercrime
  • Keep your devices and software updated
  • Back up your important data regularly

Resources and Further Help

For more information about your digital rights in Nepal, contact:

  • Nepal Police Cyber Bureau
  • Nepal's Office of the Privacy Commissioner
  • Local human rights organizations
  • Consumer Protection Organizations
Remember: Your rights online are just as important as your rights offline. Stay informed, stay safe, and stand up for your digital rights.
